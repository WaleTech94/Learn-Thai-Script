Tone route

Start with the written route: class, mark, live/dead, length, tone.

Tone route
1Initial
2Class
3Mark
4Live/dead
5Length
6Tone
  1. Find the initial consonant or cluster leader.
  2. Identify the class: mid, high, or low.
  3. Check for a tone mark.
  4. If there is no mark, decide live/dead.
  5. For low-class dead syllables, check vowel length.
  6. Name the tone.

Transliteration key
Transliteration tone signs: a mid · à low · â falling · á high · ǎ rising. These are reading hints, not Thai tone marks. Doubled vowels (aa, ii, uu) are long. or = the vowel in "for" with no r sound. bp/dt sit between English b/p and d/t.
